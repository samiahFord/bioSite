About Sara

Sara Fields was born in Sioux City, Iowa in 1956 to a loving mother, a hard-working adoring father, and a pair of twin sisters. Very shortly after, her family would grow to add two little brothers. Sara describes her early life as being “crowded” but full of love and cherished memories. A large and loving family has continued to be a major aspect of Sara’s life even up to this day. She welcomed three kids into the world with her husband Jesse. Her firstborn was a daughter named Venus, then came her only son Jesse Jr. and a while later came her youngest daughter Sarisa. The family didn’t stop there though. Sara’s kids went on to add a dozen more members to the family who Sara loved helping raise and nurture throughout the years. Family is such an important aspect of her life that she lists it as one of her greatest accomplishments! “[Family is] what I remember most fondly and what I look forward to the most”.

Alongside raising a family, Sara has had a very lengthy and accomplishment-filled career. Sara adopted her father’s amazing work ethic at a very young age when she started working as a hostess in a local diner at the age of ten. In the years that would follow, Sara would go on to start as a receptionist at Wellmark Blue Cross and Blue Shield and work her way all the way up to being the Director of Policy for the Great Plains region. Sara has since retired from that position but her work ethic brought her back into the workforce years later as a Customer representative for another insurance company to continue her passion for helping people in their greatest time of need.

In her free time, Sara loves to spend time with her family. Playing cards with her brothers and sisters or hanging out and playing with her grandchildren are her favorite pastimes but she has also recently taken on a passion project. Sara noticed a growing number of plastic bags around her and her friend’s houses and came up with an idea of how to recycle them. Having had some other family members experience homelessness, she thought of the idea to crochet the bags in a way that would produce a plush and waterproof surface for a person to sit or sleep on. The process of making one of these mats is extremely time-consuming so any time she can spare for the project is used wisely!
